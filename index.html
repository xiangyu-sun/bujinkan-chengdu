Bujinkan Chengdu & Budo Taijutsu

Bujinkan

The Bujinkan (武神館) is an international martial arts organization based in Japan and headed by Masaaki Hatsumi. The combat system taught by this organization comprises nine separate ryūha, or schools, which are collectively referred to as Bujinkan Budō Taijutsu. The Bujinkan is most commonly associated with ninjutsu. However, Masaaki Hatsumi uses the term Budo (meaning martial way) as he says the ryūha are descended from historical samurai schools that teach samurai martial tactics and ninjutsu schools that teach ninja tactics.

This training is conducted in a manner in which there are predefined "attackers" (tori) and "receivers" (uke) similar to drills in judo (gokyo) or traditional Japanese martial arts. However, the Bujinkan differs from many traditional martial arts in that the training progresses through the following stages:

  • Predefined sets of movements (kata) and physical conditioning
  • Variations to the fixed drills (henka), responding to changes in the attacker's movements or situation
  • Free form training (randori) consisting mostly of spur-of-the-moment, dynamic techniques during which the defender hits, locks, chokes or throws the attacker in a controlled and safe manner
  • More advanced training consists of controlling the attacker's mind using various techniques such as pain compliance and deception

Taijutsu

Taijutsu (body combat art) is the Bujinkan system of unarmed defence using strikes, throws, holds, chokes and joint locks. It encompases skill such as: koppo jutsu is the "way of attacking and/or using the skeletal structure"; “koshi jutsu” is the way of attacking muscles and weak points on the body; jutai jutsu is the "relaxed body method" teaching throwing, grappling and choking techniques and dakentai jutsu which emphasises strikes, kicks and blocks The first levels of training, such as leaping, tumbling, break fall techniques and body conditioning, form the basis for taijutsu. They are needed to progress into other techniques such as unarmed combat and the use of tools and weapons. Once learned, Taijutsu techniques can be applied to any situation, armed or unarmed.

Bujinkan Chengdu

Welcome to Chengdu Bujinkan budo Taijutsu!

Bujinkan Budo Taijutsu is a series of martial arts schools (ryu) that have been complied and are currently being “transmitted” in Japan by Soke Hatsumi Masaaki. A majority of the schools are traditional samurai fighting arts that have been handed down for generations.

We teach adult classes on Mondays and Wednesdays and kids classes on Wednesdays. During the summer we often meet outdoors to train on the weekends.

We use weapons, we do throws. We do some groundwork, though not much as we primarily focus on how to get back to standing as fast as possible as most of the art was developed on a battlefield with multiple opponents trying to kill each other. We do striking which includes but isn't limited to, punching, kicking, knees, elbows and other body strikes.

Expect to start with learning how to roll and hit the floor without getting injured, then move into striking, then releases from grabs, then joint locks such as wrist twists, and elbow locks, and then throws. We always relearn the basics and as a whole (teacher and students alike), we help each other get to where we need to be. Expect a relatively safe environment with great and fun people. Expect to make friends for a lifetime.

We look forward to meeting you soon!

Robert came to China with a school student exchange program when he was 12, he has taken a great interest in Chineses culture and Estern culture since then. This is one of the most important reasons he chose to live in China now. Robert is now living in Chengdu, and teaching regular weekly budo taijutsu classes.

Bujinkan Budo Taijutsu is a martial art based on whole body movement. It focuses on using effective techniques against an opponent’s weak points to efficiently negate a dangerous encounter. The student learns how to use the entire body (armed or unarmed) in execution of these techniques making it ideal against larger and stronger opponents. Budo Taijutsu is great for self defense and for conditioning of the body, the mind, of the warrior heart and of the immovable heart (不動心).
